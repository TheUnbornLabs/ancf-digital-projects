Emotional Blackmail Detector

Society · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~15 min · detector

Emotional blackmail uses fear, obligation, and guilt — "FOG" — as leverage. Paste a message to notice those patterns, for your own clarity, with a grounding reframe for each. This is a reflection aid, not a diagnosis: a flag does not prove anyone is mistreating you, a message with none can still feel wrong, and you know your relationship best.

If a relationship leaves you feeling persistently frightened, controlled, or unsafe, you deserve support. Consider talking it through with someone you trust or a qualified professional. This tool can't assess your situation — only you, and the people supporting you, can.

Key takeaway

Naming a pressure tactic is the first step to loosening its hold. Fear, obligation, and guilt work best when they're invisible — so simply seeing them at work can return a little clarity and choice. Their feelings are real and worth compassion; and they are theirs to manage, not a debt you must pay with your life decisions.
