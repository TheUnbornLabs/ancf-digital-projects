PROJECT 088

Anti-Harassment Rules Page

Community Tools · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~9 min

A safe community needs clear rules that protect everyone — members and visitors, whatever their background or views. These are copyable anti-harassment rules with a simple reporting process you can adapt.

What you will learn

  • What clear, fair anti-harassment rules look like.
  • Why a reporting process matters as much as the rules.
  • How to protect every member equally.

1. The rules (copy & adapt)

2. What harassment can look like

Personal attacksInsults, name-calling, or demeaning someone instead of addressing ideas. Targeting identityHostility based on someone's gender, religion, caste, ethnicity, disability, orientation, or background — never acceptable. Pressure & intimidationThreats, stalking, or repeated unwanted contact. Disrespecting choicesShaming people for their reproductive choices — in any direction.

3. Reporting process

1. ReportMembers can report privately to moderators at [contact]. 2. AcknowledgeModerators respond promptly and treat reports seriously and discreetly. 3. ReviewBoth sides are considered fairly before any action. 4. ActClear, proportionate steps — a reminder, warning, or removal as needed.

4. Quick check

1. Good rules protect…
Only people who agree with us
Every member, whatever their background or views
Only moderators

2. A reporting process…
Is optional decoration
Makes the rules real and usable
Encourages drama

Key takeaway

Rules without a process are just words. Name what harassment looks like, protect everyone equally, and give members a clear, fair way to report — that's what makes a community genuinely safe.
