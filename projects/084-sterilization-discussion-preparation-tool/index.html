Sterilization Discussion Preparation Tool

Childfree Life · Difficulty: Intermediate

Build a clear question list to bring to a qualified provider when discussing permanent contraception. This is preparation support only — not medical advice, and it makes no recommendation. Your clinician provides the actual guidance for your situation.

1. Build your questions

2. Edit and take it with you

3. If you feel dismissed

Some people seeking permanent contraception report being dismissed or told they'll "change their mind". You're entitled to be heard respectfully. A few supportive reminders:

You can ask for your request to be documented

"Please note in my record that I asked about this and my stated preference." A clear paper trail can help.

A second opinion is reasonable

If a provider won't engage with your question, seeking another clinician is a normal, legitimate step.

Bring support and notes

A support person and a written list make it easier to stay clear and calm in the room.

None of this is medical or legal advice — it's about helping you prepare for a respectful conversation.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice. Any decision about permanent contraception must be made with a qualified clinician.
