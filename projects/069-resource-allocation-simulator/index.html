PROJECT 069

Resource Allocation Simulator

Games · Difficulty: Beginner · ~5 min

A simple toy showing how a fixed pool divides per person as a household grows. It's just division — a way to picture trade-offs. It implies nothing about anyone's worth, and real life is far richer than a fixed pie (love, after all, isn't divided).

A maths illustration only. It does not judge family size or anyone's choices, and real resources aren't a fixed pie — incomes grow, support exists, and care multiplies. Just a thinking aid.

What you will learn

  • How a fixed amount splits into smaller per-person shares as a group grows.
  • Why "more people, smaller slices" is only true when the pie is fixed.
  • How to hold a trade-off in mind without judging anyone.

Key takeaway

Division is simple; life is not. A fixed pool divides into smaller shares — but incomes, support, and love don't behave like a fixed pie. The toy is a prompt to think about trade-offs, never to judge a family.
