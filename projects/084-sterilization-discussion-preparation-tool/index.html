PROJECT 084

Sterilization Discussion Preparation Tool

Practical Tools · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~8 min

If you're considering a conversation with a provider about permanent contraception, this builds a tidy list of questions to ask them — tailored to what you most want to understand. It helps you walk in prepared and be heard.

This tool offers no recommendation for or against any procedure, and is not medical advice. It does not describe how procedures are performed. All decisions belong to you and a qualified provider. Your body, your choice — whatever you decide.

What you will learn

  • The kinds of questions worth asking a provider.
  • How to organise them by what matters to you.
  • Ways to keep the conversation calm and clear.

1. Build your question list

Tick the topics you want to ask about, then generate an editable list to take with you.

2. Keeping the talk clear

Bring it written"I've written some questions — can we go through them together?" Ask for plain terms"Could you explain that simply so I'm sure I understand?" It's okay to pause"I'd like time to think before deciding anything."

3. Quick check

1. This tool…
Recommends a procedure
Helps you prepare questions — no recommendation
Replaces a provider

2. The decision belongs to…
This website
You and a qualified provider
Your relatives

Key takeaway

A good conversation starts with good questions. Whatever you're weighing, walking in with a clear list — and the right to take your time — helps you and your provider make the decision that's yours to make.
