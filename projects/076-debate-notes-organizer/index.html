PROJECT 076

Debate Notes Organizer

Writing Tools · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~9 min

Prepare for a respectful discussion by sorting your thoughts into four boxes: your claims, their strongest points, the evidence, and the common ground. Steelmanning the other side is part of being ready. Saved on this device.

What you will learn

  • Why preparing the other side's best points makes you stronger.
  • How to separate claims from evidence.
  • Why naming common ground lowers the heat.

Key takeaway

The best-prepared person in the room can state the other side fairly. Sort your claims from your evidence, steelman the opposition, and start from common ground — that's how good discussions happen.
