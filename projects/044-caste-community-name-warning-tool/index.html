Caste/Community Name Warning Tool

Community Tools · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~7 min

When we critique pressure, it's vital we aim at systems and behaviours — never at castes, communities, religions, or ethnic groups. This tool screens a draft for generalising or targeting language and helps you rephrase. It's a safeguard for kindness, used before you post.

This tool protects groups. Our content rules forbid hatred or generalisation toward any caste, community, religion, ethnicity, or other group. Critique the structure or the specific behaviour — never the people. This is a writing aid, not a judgement of you.

What you will learn

  • How generalising language slips into otherwise fair criticism.
  • The difference between critiquing a system and targeting a group.
  • How to rephrase toward pressures, structures, and specific behaviours.

2. How to rephrase

Instead of a group…name the pressure ("the expectation that…"), the system ("policies that…"), or the specific behaviour ("when someone says…"). Instead of "they always…""some people, in some situations, do X" — and explain why the situation, not the identity, drives it. Instead of blame…describe the structure that creates the pressure, and who it harms.

3. Quick check

1. Fair criticism aims at…
A whole caste, community, or ethnic group
Systems, pressures, and specific behaviours
Whoever's easiest to blame

2. "They always do X" is…
A fair generalisation
A stereotype to avoid
Always true

Key takeaway

The strongest critique names the pressure and the system, not a people. Aiming at structures keeps your argument both fairer and harder to dismiss — and it protects the very people who may also be hurt by those structures.
