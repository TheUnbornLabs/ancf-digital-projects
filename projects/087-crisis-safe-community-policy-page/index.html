Crisis-Safe Community Policy Page

Community Tools · Difficulty: Advanced

A model policy to help a community respond safely and kindly if a member is in distress. Adapt it with your own local resources. It is guidance, not professional or legal advice — and never a replacement for emergency services.

Our stance

We never encourage self-harm or harm to others, and we never shame anyone for struggling. We meet distress with care and signposting — and we're honest that a community is not a crisis service.

The response steps

A calm, repeatable order for moderators when someone signals they're struggling.

  1. Notice & stay calm. Take it seriously; don't panic or over-react publicly.
  2. Respond with warmth, privately where possible. "I'm really glad you said something. You're not alone."
  3. Share general support resources. Point to your pre-listed local/national lines.
  4. If there's immediate danger, encourage contacting local emergency services right away.
  5. Don't amplify graphic content. Gently remove or limit detailed descriptions of methods.
  6. Never discipline the distress. No warnings or removals for reaching out — only for genuine rule-breaking.
  7. Debrief the mod team. Supporting others is heavy; check on each other afterward.

What we do — and don't

We do

Respond kindly and promptly · share resources · keep the space calm · look out for each other.

We don't

Diagnose · promise confidentiality we can't guarantee · replace professional help · punish someone for being in pain.

Adopt this policy

Copy a clean text version to adapt for your own group. Remember to insert real, region-appropriate support lines.
