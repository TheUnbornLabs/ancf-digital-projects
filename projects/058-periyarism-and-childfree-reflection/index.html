PROJECT 058

Periyarism & Childfree Reflection

Society · Difficulty: Advanced · ~20 min · reflection notes

Periyar E. V. Ramasamy led the rationalist Self-Respect Movement in twentieth-century Tamil Nadu, emphasising reason, dignity, anti-caste justice, and — especially — women's autonomy. These notes connect his ideas to childfree reflection. He was a social reformer, not an antinatalist; the link here is his insistence that no one should be bound by imposed roles they did not choose.

Key takeaway

Periyar's enduring challenge was simple and radical: don't accept a custom merely because it's old — examine it by reason and dignity, and free people, especially women, from roles imposed without consent. Whatever one concludes about children, that spirit of self-respect and chosen, not compelled, living is the through-line to a childfree reflection.
