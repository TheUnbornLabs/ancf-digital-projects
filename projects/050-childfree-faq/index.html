Childfree FAQ

Childfree Life · Difficulty: Beginner

The questions childfree people hear most, answered calmly and respectfully. Search for a topic, expand any answer, and copy one if it helps you reply to someone.

Browse the FAQ

Isn't a childfree life lonely?

No more than any life. Loneliness comes from a lack of connection, not a lack of children. Friendships, partnership, community, and purpose all provide it — and many parents feel lonely too. A life full of chosen relationships is a full life.

Will you regret it?

Some childfree people do; many don't — and exactly the same is true of becoming a parent. Regret isn't unique to either path. What protects against it is making a thoughtful, honest decision, which is precisely what you're doing.

Who will care for you when you're older?

Care in later life comes from planning, savings, friendships, chosen family, and services — not automatically from children. Many older parents are cared for by professionals, not their kids. Planning your own support directly is wise for everyone.

Do you dislike children?

Usually not at all. Plenty of childfree people adore their nieces, nephews, students, and friends' kids. Not wanting to raise your own is simply different from not liking children.

Isn't it selfish?

Choosing the life that genuinely fits you isn't selfish — and selfishness is about harming others for gain, which this choice doesn't do. Many childfree people pour care into friends, community, work, and causes.

What if you change your mind?

Then you'll make a new decision with the information you have then — that's true of every major choice. A considered "no" now isn't invalidated by the possibility of change later.

Isn't it unnatural?

People build meaningful lives in countless ways, and "different from the common path" isn't the same as "wrong." Plenty of human choices depart from nature; that alone doesn't settle whether they're good.

Don't you want a "real" family?

A family is made by love and commitment, not by a headcount. A couple, close friends, a person and their community — these are all real families. Mine just looks the way I chose it to.
