Buddhism & Antinatalism Notes

Philosophy · Difficulty: Advanced · ~20 min · study notes

Respectful study notes on Buddhist themes alongside questions about birth. Buddhism is a vast, living tradition followed by hundreds of millions — and it is not antinatalist. These notes explore the core concepts accurately, bust a common myth, and map honestly where the two views touch and where they part. They speak about ideas, never for any practitioner.

Key takeaway

Buddhism takes suffering seriously — the First Noble Truth — yet answers it with a path to liberation and boundless compassion, not with a case against birth. Some thinkers note resonances, but the tradition itself, lay and monastic alike, is not antinatalist. Engage its depth on its own terms, without flattening it into a slogan.
