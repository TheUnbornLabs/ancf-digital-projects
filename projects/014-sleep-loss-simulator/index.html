Sleep Loss Simulator

Calculators · Difficulty: Beginner

An illustrative model of cumulative sleep debt over a stretch of short nights. It updates live and is educational only — sleep is complex, and this is a simple arithmetic picture, not medical advice or a real diagnosis of tiredness.

How it's calculated:

nightly_deficit = need − actual total_debt = nightly_deficit × nights full_nights_missed = total_debt / need

How to read this

The "first year with a newborn" preset uses a commonly cited rough figure — many new parents report losing on the order of an hour or two of sleep a night in the early months. It is an illustration, not a measurement of any particular family; with shared night-duties and a good sleeper, the real number can be far smaller.

Sleep loss is one honest consideration among many. It is not a verdict on parenthood, and plenty of parents find the trade worth it.

Caveats

Sleep debt isn't simple addition

The body doesn't bank or repay lost sleep hour-for-hour. This linear model overstates how neatly debt accumulates and how cleanly it can be "paid back".

Individual variation is huge

Sleep need, resilience, naps, and recovery differ from person to person. Treat the output as a rough shape, not a personal health metric.

If you're genuinely struggling

Persistent sleep loss can affect health and mood. If that's you, this toy can't help — please speak with a qualified professional.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
