A flagged phrase is a prompt to reflect, not proof of intent. Many people use these patterns without realising it.

Patterns this looks for

Guilt over past sacrifices

"After all we did for you…" — turning love and care into a debt you must repay by complying.

Fear of the future

"You'll regret it / be sorry" — using predicted unhappiness to pressure a decision now.

Conditional love

"If you loved us, you'd…" — making affection depend on doing what they want.

Shame & social image

"What will people think?" — leveraging reputation and embarrassment.

Emotional escalation

"You'll break my heart / be the death of me" — raising the stakes to make refusal feel cruel.

If this is your relationship

Noticing these patterns can be unsettling, especially from people you love. You're allowed to hold a boundary even when someone is upset, and someone else's strong feelings are not your responsibility to fix by giving in. If a relationship consistently uses guilt or fear to control you, please consider talking it through with someone you trust or a qualified professional. You deserve support and respect.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
