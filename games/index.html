100 ANCF Games

One hundred small browser games built around agency, consent, boundaries, and self-respect. Each one is a single self-contained page — click it and it runs. Nothing to install, nothing to sign up for, nothing that follows you afterwards.

The themes here critique social pressure, coercion, and the life script — never children, parents, or people. Play them as a lighter door into the same questions the rest of the archive asks slowly.

How this works

Every game is one HTML file: the art is drawn on a canvas, the sound is generated in the browser, and there are no external libraries or requests. Once a page has loaded it keeps working without a connection, and you can save it and play it offline.

High scores and the played/unplayed markers on this page live in your browser's local storage. Saved on this device only. Nothing is uploaded. Clearing your browser data clears them, and the Reset button above clears the played markers here.

Most games are keyboard-and-mouse first with touch controls on phones. They are meant to be short — a few minutes each, not an evening.
