PROJECT 045

Identity Pressure Reflection Page

Society · Difficulty: Beginner · ~15 min · reflection

Belonging is precious — and sometimes it carries expectations about children. This page helps you notice how different parts of your identity (family role, gender, culture, faith, class, peers, and the voice in your own head) carry pressure. Rate each, see where it's loudest for you, and reflect. There are no right answers here — only your own.

① Map your pressures

For each part of who you are, how much child-related expectation does it carry for you? (0 = none, 5 = a great deal.) Your answers stay on this device.

② Your pressure picture

Sorted from loudest to quietest. This is a snapshot of where the weight sits — not a verdict, just a clearer view.

③ Understanding each part

How each part of identity tends to shape expectations. Tap to expand.

④ Your reflection

Saved on this device only.

Key takeaway

The expectations we carry rarely come from one place — they're woven through family, gender, culture, faith, and the stories we tell ourselves. Seeing which threads pull hardest is the first step to deciding which ones you want to keep weaving, and which you can gently set down. Belonging and self-determination can coexist.
