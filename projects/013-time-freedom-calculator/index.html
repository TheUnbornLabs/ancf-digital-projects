PROJECT 013

Time-Freedom Calculator

Calculators · Difficulty: Beginner · ~12 min · editable model

Every week holds 168 hours. This tool shows how yours divide, how much discretionary time is left, how adding caregiving changes the picture, and what your free hours add up to over a year, a decade, a lifetime. Drag the sliders to your own reality. Time isn't the only thing that matters in any life choice — but it's one of the most honest, and the easiest to overlook.

① The idea

Discretionary time — the hours left after the non-negotiables: sleep, work, commuting, chores, personal care, eating, and any caregiving. It's the raw material of rest, relationships, creativity, and growth — and it's finite. Seeing it as a number makes trade-offs visible.

Nothing is uploaded; your week is saved only on this device.

Key takeaway

Time is the one budget no one can top up. Whatever you choose, the question worth asking isn't only "how many free hours do I have?" but "am I spending them on purpose?" Seeing the week as 168 finite hours turns a vague sense of busyness into something you can actually steer.
