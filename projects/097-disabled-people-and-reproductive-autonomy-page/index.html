PROJECT 097

Disabled People and Reproductive Autonomy Page

Ethics · Difficulty: Advanced

A respectful page centring disabled people's autonomy and dignity, and opposing coercion in every direction. It is educational only — never advice about anyone's specific situation, and never a judgement about any individual's choices.

What this page affirms

Autonomy belongs to everyone

Disabled people have exactly the same right as anyone to decide whether to have children — to choose parenthood or a childfree life. Disability does not reduce that right, and no one else gets to make the call.

Vigilance against coercion

History includes coercive practices against disabled people, including forced or pressured sterilisation. That history is a reason for real vigilance: autonomy means freedom from being pushed either way.

Coercion runs in both directions

Some disabled people face pressure not to have children; others are denied the support that would make parenting possible. Both undermine choice. Genuine autonomy includes the support to choose either path.

Nothing about us without us

Disabled people are the authorities on their own lives. Conversations about disability and reproduction should centre disabled voices, not speak over them.

Support, not pressure

The goal is real choice backed by real support — accessible healthcare, clear information, and respect — free from judgement in any direction. A society that genuinely respects autonomy makes every path liveable, and lets each person decide for themselves.
