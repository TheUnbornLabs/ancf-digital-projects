Stoicism & Antinatalism Notes

Philosophy · Difficulty: Advanced · ~20 min · study notes

Study notes putting Stoic ideas in conversation with questions about birth. Importantly, the Stoics were not antinatalists — most valued family, friendship, and civic life as natural goods. These notes explore the genuine concepts, bust a common myth, and map honestly where the two views converge and where they part.

Key takeaway

Stoicism offers powerful tools for equanimity — but it was a life-affirming, pro-social philosophy, not a case against birth. Reading it honestly means taking what genuinely helps (calm in the face of pressure, focus on what's yours to control) without conscripting Epictetus or Marcus into an argument they never made.
