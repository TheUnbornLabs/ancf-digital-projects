Crisis-Safe Community Policy Page

Community Tools · Difficulty: Advanced · ~10 min

If you run a community that discusses heavy topics, it helps to agree — in advance — how moderators respond when someone seems to be in crisis. This is a model policy you can adapt and copy, plus a simple readiness checklist.

This is general guidance, not professional or clinical advice. Moderators are not crisis counsellors. The goal is to respond with care and connect people to real help — fast.

What you will learn

  • What a calm, safe crisis-response policy contains.
  • The role of a moderator — and its limits.
  • Why pre-agreed steps beat improvising in the moment.

1. Model policy (copy & adapt)

A starting template. Replace the bracketed parts with your own local resources, then copy it into your community's rules.

2. Moderator response steps

1. Stay calm & kindRespond gently and without judgment. Thank the person for sharing. 2. Don't counselYou're a caring human, not a clinician. Avoid diagnosing or advising. 3. Signpost supportShare local crisis lines and encourage contact with a professional or trusted person. 4. EmergenciesIf someone may be in immediate danger, point to local emergency services right away. 5. Protect the spaceMove on gently; avoid graphic detail; follow up privately if appropriate.

Key takeaway

You can't counsel, but you can care — and caring well means having a plan. A short, pre-agreed policy lets moderators respond calmly, point to real support, and keep the whole community safe.
