PROJECT 086

Mental Health Boundary Notice

Community Tools · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~8 min

Online spaces about big life topics can get heavy. This helps you draft a short, caring notice — for a group, post, or thread — that sets a gentle boundary and points people toward real support. The tone is warmth, not warning.

This is a communication aid, not a substitute for professional help. If you or someone else may be in immediate danger, contact local emergency services or a crisis line in your country right away.

What you will learn

  • How to set a caring boundary without shutting people out.
  • Why signposting support matters in any community space.
  • Language that stays warm and non-judgmental.

1. Build your notice

Add your own local helpline details where the notice marks [local support]. Helplines differ by country, so listing the right ones for your members is best.

2. A few principles

Care, not controlA boundary protects everyone's wellbeing; it isn't a telling-off. Always signpostPoint to professional and local crisis support — never imply the group can replace it. Stay non-judgmentalPeople share when they feel safe, not policed.

3. Quick check

1. A good boundary notice mainly conveys…
A telling-off
Care, plus where to find real support
That heavy topics are banned

2. A community group can…
Replace professional help
Support people and point to professional help
Ignore wellbeing

Key takeaway

The kindest community spaces hold two things at once: warmth for hard conversations, and a clear pointer to support beyond the group. Set the boundary with care, signpost real help, and people feel safe rather than silenced.
