Parenthood Assumption Quiz

Society · Difficulty: Beginner

There are no wrong people in this quiz — only assumptions to notice. Pick the option closest to your honest first reaction, then read the explanations.

Why "assumptions", not "answers"?

Everyday language about parenthood is full of quiet assumptions — that "family" means children, that being childfree is temporary, that asking about someone's reproductive plans is small talk. None of these make anyone a bad person; we all absorbed them. This quiz just helps you spot them, in others and in yourself. There are no wrong people here — only ideas to weigh.

Assumptions hiding in everyday phrases

Before the quiz, a quick reference. Each common phrase carries an unspoken assumption — naming it is the whole skill.

"Starting a family"Assumes a family only begins with children — though a couple or a chosen household is already a family. "Do you have kids yet?"The "yet" assumes children are coming, treating childfree life as an unfinished state. "When are you settling down?"Bundles partnership, home, and children into one expected package on a fixed timeline. "It's different with your own"Assumes the speaker knows your future feelings better than you do. "You'd make such a good parent"A compliment that quietly assumes parenthood is the goal you must be aiming at.

'You’ll regret it.'
'That’s a big decision — what feels right for you?'
'Who will care for you when you’re old?'

Having children

The person works too hard

They probably have or want children

6. A friend is childless after wanting children. A caring response is…
"At least you can travel!"
"Everything happens for a reason."

0 of 6 answered.

The takeaway

Noticing an assumption is not about catching people out — it is about giving everyone, including yourself, a little more room. The most respectful default is curiosity: ask, listen, and leave space for an answer you didn't expect.
