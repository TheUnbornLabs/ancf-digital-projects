Personal Manifesto Builder

Writing Tools · Difficulty: Beginner

A manifesto is just a clear statement of what you stand for. Choose a central value and a tone to draft a short one, then make it your own — words you could return to when the pressure gets loud.

1. Draft your manifesto

Your manifesto will appear here.

2. Make it yours

3. Tips

Write it in the present tense

"I choose…", "I hold…" — present-tense lines feel owned, not aspirational. You're describing who you already are.

Keep one core value

A manifesto is stronger for being focused. Pick the value that matters most and build around it.

End with respect for others

A line like "I respect other paths, and ask the same for mine" keeps the manifesto firm without being combative.
