Family Function Survival Script

Writing Tools · Difficulty: Beginner · ~8 min

Big gatherings go better with a plan. Assemble a small pocket script — a calm opener, a deflection, a firm line, and a graceful exit — and tick a readiness checklist so you walk in steady. Saved on this device.

What you will learn

  • The four lines worth having ready before a gathering.
  • How to deflect without conflict.
  • How to exit a conversation gracefully.
  • How small preparation lowers stress.

Key takeaway

You don't need a speech — just four steady lines and an exit. A little preparation turns a dreaded gathering into something you can move through calmly, on your own terms.
