Writing Tools · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~12 min · generator

Scaffold a fair, well-structured article outline. Pick a topic and a framing, and the outline includes built-in space for the opposing view — because the strongest writing takes the other side seriously. You get a hook, a thesis, body sections, a steelman of the counter-case, and a considered conclusion. Edit and copy.

Key takeaway

An outline that reserves real space for the other side does two things: it makes your own argument stronger by stress-testing it, and it earns the reader's trust by showing you're not hiding from the hard parts. Fairness isn't a weakness in persuasion — it's the foundation of it.
