# Parenthood Assumption Quiz

*Project 004 · Category: Society · Difficulty: Intermediate*

A 360-degree interactive toolkit for examining the quiet assumptions behind everyday
parenthood talk — naming them, testing whether they hold, tracing where they came from,
and deciding which to keep. The companion to Project 003 (which handles pressure from
others); this one turns the lens inward, on your own inherited beliefs.

Eight modules:
1. **What assumptions are** — how they hide (default thinking, social proof, status-quo
   bias, description-vs-prescription) and the one move that matters: examine, don't reject.
2. **Spot the assumption** — an 8-card decoder; name the family, then see the hidden premise
   and a one-line way to examine it.
3. **Assumption library** — a searchable, filterable catalogue of 18 common assumptions,
   each with a fair two-sided look and a reflective question.
4. **Your profile** — rate ten assumption families; see a radar chart and tailored insight.
5. **Belief examiner** — a five-step Socratic walkthrough of a single belief, ending in a
   personal, copyable summary.
6. **Trace the roots** — map where your strongest assumption took hold.
7. **Quick check** — a six-question quiz.
8. **Reflection** — private prompts saved on-device.

All vanilla JS on the shared `../../ancf-ui.js` toolkit; verified with a headless DOM test
(20/20). Nothing is uploaded; ratings and reflections live only in the browser.

> This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or
> mental health advice.
