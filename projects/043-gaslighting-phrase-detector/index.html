Gaslighting Phrase Detector

Society · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~7 min

Gaslighting is a pattern of making someone doubt their own memory or perception. This tool flags phrases associated with that pattern, to help you trust your own read of a situation. It can't diagnose a relationship — only you can — but naming the pattern can help.

An awareness aid, not a diagnosis. A single phrase is not proof of anything. If you often feel confused or doubt your reality after talking to someone, that matters — please consider support from someone you trust or a qualified professional. You deserve to feel safe.

What you will learn

  • Four patterns associated with gaslighting: denial, minimising, blame-shifting, and reality-doubt.
  • Why trusting your own perception is protective.
  • When to seek outside support.

Key takeaway

Your perception is valid information. Naming reality-denying patterns can help you hold onto it. If someone repeatedly leaves you doubting what you saw and felt, that pattern is worth taking seriously — and reaching for support is wise, not weak.
