PROJECT 023

Ethics Flashcards

Learning Tools · Difficulty: Beginner

Twelve key terms in birth ethics and autonomy, each with a short, neutral definition. Tap or press Enter on a card to flip it. Shuffle for a fresh order, then use the self-test below to track what you can define from memory — great for study circles.

Flashcards

Tap a card — or focus it and press Enter / Space — to flip.

Antinatalism
The view that creating new life is ethically worth questioning.
Pronatalism
The social assumption that people should have children.
Autonomy
A person's right to govern their own body and life choices.
Asymmetry argument
A claim that absent suffering is good while absent pleasure is not bad.
Consent
Informed agreement — central to many autonomy debates.
Childfree
Choosing, by preference, not to have children.
Coercion
Pressure that undermines a free choice.
Reproductive justice
A framework linking autonomy with social conditions.
Non-identity problem
A puzzle about "harming" a person who exists only because of the act.
Suffering-risk
The unavoidable possibility of harm contained in any life.
Negative utilitarianism
An ethics prioritising the reduction of suffering over adding pleasure.
Bodily autonomy
A person's final say over what happens to their own body.

Self-test

Without flipping, tick each term you can define from memory. Progress saves on this device.
