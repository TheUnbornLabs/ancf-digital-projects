PROJECT 014

Sleep-Loss Simulator

Calculators · Difficulty: Beginner · ~10 min · illustrative model

Early caregiving often means months of reduced sleep. This simple model shows how a nightly shortfall adds up over time into a cumulative sleep debt. It's purely illustrative — every baby and family is different, and many parents will tell you it's a price they'd pay again gladly. The point isn't to frighten or to argue; it's to make one real, under-discussed cost visible so it can be planned for.

① About the model

Sleep debt — the running total of sleep you miss versus what your body needs. It builds fastest in the newborn months and eases as a child sleeps longer. This model multiplies an average nightly shortfall by the nights in each phase. It is not a medical tool and not a prediction for any real family.

Adjust the nightly shortfall for each phase below to match what you expect or have heard; the defaults are rough, commonly-cited ballparks.

Key takeaway

Sleep is a real, rarely-budgeted cost of early caregiving — and like most costs, it's most manageable when it's expected rather than a shock. Seeing the rough shape of it helps parents plan support, and helps anyone weighing the choice add an honest line to the ledger. It's a peak that passes; naming it just makes it easier to carry or to weigh.
