Marriage & Children Conversation Tool

Community Tools · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~20 min · couple tool

Few conversations matter more for a couple than the one about children. This tool helps you map where you and your partner align across the key questions, see it clearly, and prepare for an honest, kind talk — including real help for the hardest case, where you don't agree. Best done together; everything stays on this device.

① Before you start

The aim — not to score the relationship, but to make the implicit explicit. Many couples discover they agree more than they feared, or surface a real difference early enough to handle it with care. Answer honestly; a tool can only help if it's told the truth.

④ The conversation

A gentle agenda for the actual talk. Add your own notes; they're saved on this device.

1. Set the tone. "I love you and I want us to be honest about this, with no pressure either way."
2. Share, don't sell. Each says their view and the feeling under it, uninterrupted.
3. Find the overlap. Start with the questions you align on — most couples have many.
4. Name the gaps. Use section ③; treat them as shared problems, not opposing sides.
5. Decide the next step, not the whole future. Another talk, more thinking, or help from a professional.

⑤ If you don't agree

The hard, honest part

  • A child isn't a compromise. "Half a child" doesn't exist; one person ends up living the other's answer. That's worth facing squarely, not papering over.
  • Wearing someone down isn't winning. A "yes" extracted by pressure, or a resentful "fine," tends to cost the relationship later — and is unfair to a child.
  • Timing differences aren't the same as a true mismatch. "Not yet" and "not ever" are very different; name which one you each mean.
  • Get help early. A couples counselor is not a last resort; for a difference this size, it's wisdom. (See also The Childfree Life, Ch. 22.)
  • Sometimes loving people part over this — kindly, and without either being the villain. That, too, can be the most respectful outcome.

⑥ Your reflection

Saved on this device only.

Key takeaway

The children question rewards couples who face it early, honestly, and together — as teammates solving a shared puzzle, not opponents defending positions. Alignment is a gift; a difference found early is still far better than one found late. Either way, the conversation itself is an act of love.
