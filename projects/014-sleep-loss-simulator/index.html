PROJECT 014

Sleep Loss Simulator

Calculators · Difficulty: Beginner · ~6 min

Early caregiving often means months of reduced sleep. This simple model shows how a nightly shortfall adds up over time into a cumulative "sleep debt". It is purely illustrative — every family's experience differs, and many find the trade-off well worth it.

Educational illustration only — not medical advice. Sleep needs and effects vary by person. If sleep problems are affecting your health, please speak with a qualified professional.

What you will learn

  • How a small nightly shortfall compounds over weeks and months.
  • How to express sleep debt in relatable units (full nights).
  • Why cumulative effects can be larger than they feel night to night.
  • That this is illustrative, and rest can be recovered and supported.

Key takeaway

Cumulative effects can be far larger than they feel night to night — which is exactly why support, shared load, and recovery matter so much. This is a prompt for honest planning and compassion, not a reason for fear or guilt.
