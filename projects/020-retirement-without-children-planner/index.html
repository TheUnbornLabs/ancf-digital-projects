Retirement Without Children Planner

Calculators · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~18 min · planner

"Who will look after you when you're old?" is better answered by planning than by having children — who are, in any case, never a guarantee of care. This planner does two things: it projects how savings might grow toward later-life security, and it helps you build the care network that money alone can't provide. Not financial advice — a tool for thinking ahead.

① The real question② Your inputs③ Projection④ Beyond money⑤ Quick check⑥ Reflection

① The real question

Two kinds of security — later-life wellbeing rests on resources (savings, insurance, a home) and on relationships (people and services who show up). Children are an unreliable route to either; a deliberate plan beats a hopeful one. This tool takes both halves seriously.

Key takeaway

The honest answer to "who will look after you?" isn't "my children" — it's "the plan and the people I build." Resources grow with small, steady steps; a care network grows with attention to relationships over years. Both are within your hands far more than a roll of the genetic dice ever was.
