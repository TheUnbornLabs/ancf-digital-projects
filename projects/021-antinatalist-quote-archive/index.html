PROJECT 021

Antinatalist Quote Archive

Learning Tools · Difficulty: Beginner

These are short, original paraphrased study prompts that capture recurring themes in antinatalist thought — written for reflection and discussion. They are not quotations and are not attributed to any person. For real sources, see the reading list at the bottom.

Browse the prompts

asymmetry “To weigh a life fairly, count both the joys it gains and the harms it cannot avoid.”
consent “A choice that binds another forever deserves more care than one that binds only ourselves.”
compassion “Compassion can mean preventing suffering, not only comforting it.”
respect “Questioning a tradition is not the same as condemning those who keep it.”
love “The absence of a person is not the absence of love.”
non-identity “No one waits in the wings, disappointed at not being born.”
risk “To gamble with a life is easy; to be the chip is not.”
autonomy “A life unchosen can still be embraced — but it was never asked for.”
humility “Certainty about another's future happiness is a debt we promise on their behalf.”
care “Caring about the future need not mean filling it with new people.”

Where these ideas come from — real sources to read

Unlike the prompts above, these are genuine, verifiable works. Read the originals to judge the arguments for yourself.

David BenatarBetter Never to Have Been: The Harm of Coming into Existence (2006) — the modern asymmetry argument. Arthur SchopenhauerThe World as Will and Representation; Studies in Pessimism — 19th-century philosophical pessimism. Emil CioranThe Trouble with Being Born (1973) — aphoristic reflections on existence. Peter Wessel ZapffeThe Last Messiah (1933) — an early existential statement of the view. Sarah PerryEvery Cradle Is a Grave (2014) — a contemporary treatment.
