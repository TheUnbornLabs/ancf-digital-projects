Respectful Debate Guide

Community Tools · Difficulty: Beginner · ~15 min · guide + tools

Hard topics deserve careful conversation. This guide offers principles for disagreeing well, a quick self-audit of your own debate habits, and a phrase-swap library that turns heat into light — so you can protect both the conversation and the relationship at the same time.

① The principles

Six habits that keep a disagreement productive. Tap each to expand.

② Self-audit

Rate how true each statement is of you in a heated discussion (1 = rarely, 5 = almost always). Honest answers only — this is just for you.

③ Phrase swaps

Same point, less heat. Tap any swap to copy the better version.

④ Quick check

Two quick questions — educational, not a test.

1. To "steelman" an argument means to…
Restate it in its strongest, fairest form before replying
Attack its weakest version
Ignore it

2. The goal of respectful debate is usually to…
Understand and be understood, not just to win
Win at any cost
Never disagree

⑤ Your reflection

Saved on this device only.

Key takeaway

You can disagree completely and still treat someone well — in fact, that's the only kind of disagreement that ever changes anything. Aim at ideas, steelman before you answer, stay curious, and pick words that lower the temperature. The relationship usually matters more than the point.
