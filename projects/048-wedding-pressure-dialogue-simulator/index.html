PROJECT 048

Wedding Pressure Dialogue Simulator

Society · Difficulty: Intermediate

Weddings are peak season for "when are you next?" Walk through a few common moments, peek at a sample reply if you're stuck, and draft your own calm line. Rehearsing once makes the real moment far easier. Private to this device.

Scene 1 — "When are you next?"

A relative at the wedding asks when you're "next". Draft your warm, brief reply.

Show a sample reply

"Ha! No plans on that front — we're just here to enjoy this wedding. How's your family doing?"

Scene 2 — "Your home will be so empty"

Someone jokes that your home will be "empty". How do you respond with grace?

Show a sample reply

"Honestly, it's pretty full — of friends, plans, and a fair amount of mess. We like it that way."

Scene 3 — Unsolicited advice from an elder

An elder offers unsolicited advice. Write a line that thanks them and holds your boundary.

Show a sample reply

"Thank you — I know that comes from caring about me. We've thought it through, and we're happy with where we are."

Saved only on this device.

Read your replies out loud once or twice — rehearsal is what makes them feel natural in the moment.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
