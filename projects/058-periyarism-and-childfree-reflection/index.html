PROJECT 058

Periyarism and Childfree Reflection

Society · Difficulty: Advanced · ~10 min

Periyar E. V. Ramasamy led the rationalist Self-Respect Movement in twentieth-century Tamil Nadu, emphasising reason, dignity, social justice, and especially women's self-respect and self-determination. These notes reflect on how those values resonate with reproductive autonomy — respectfully, and without speaking for any community.

An educational reflection, not a political statement or a claim to represent any group. Periyarism is a broad social-reform tradition, not specifically a position on having children. Read primary and scholarly sources for depth.

What you will learn

  • A few core values of the Self-Respect Movement.
  • How rationalism and self-respect connect to personal autonomy.
  • Why this is a resonance, not an equation.

1. Core values (briefly)

Self-respect

The dignity and worth of every individual, independent of birth, caste, or custom — and the right to live by one's own reasoned choices.

Rationalism

Questioning superstition and inherited authority; asking "why?" of customs rather than obeying them automatically.

Social justice

Opposition to caste hierarchy and discrimination, and advocacy for the dignity of marginalised people.

Women's self-determination

A strong emphasis on women's freedom, education, and the right to make their own life decisions.

2. Resonances with reproductive autonomy

Question inherited normsRationalism invites examining the assumption that everyone must marry and have children. Self-respect & choiceThe dignity to decide one's own path aligns naturally with the freedom to choose whether to have children. Women's autonomyA focus on women's self-determination connects directly to reproductive autonomy.

3. A resonance, not an equation

Periyarism is a wide social-reform tradition with many emphases; it is not a doctrine about family size, and people who hold its values land in many different places on having children. The honest connection here is one of method and dignity — reason, self-respect, and autonomy — not a shared conclusion about birth.

Key takeaway

Reason, dignity, and self-respect are a method, not a verdict. They invite each person to examine inherited norms and decide for themselves — which is exactly what reproductive autonomy protects, in every direction.
