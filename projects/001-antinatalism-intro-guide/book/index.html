The Antinatalism Reader

A complete, beginner-friendly education in the philosophy that asks whether bringing a new person into existence is something we should do — presented fairly, with every argument met by its strongest objection.

Seven parts · 43 chapters · arguments, thinkers, varieties, objections, and living with the question

Start reading →Back to the interactive guide

How to use this book

  • It reads front to back, but every chapter stands on its own — follow the table of contents to whatever draws you.
  • Use the arrow keys (← →) or the buttons at the foot of each chapter to move between chapters; a progress bar at the top tracks your place.
  • Each argument is given in its strongest form, then immediately met with the best objection to it. Boxes marked Objection and Reply show the live disagreement.
  • It is written from inside the antinatalist conversation, sympathetically — but it is a teaching text, not persuasion. The conclusion is yours to reach.
This book is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental-health advice. If the subject touches something painful for you personally, please be gentle with yourself, and see Chapter 31, which addresses this directly.

Before you begin

Whether you arrive curious, skeptical, sympathetic, or unsettled, you are welcome here. The aim is not to recruit you but to equip you — to let you understand one of philosophy's most provocative questions well enough to make up your own mind, and to treat both those who hold the view and those who choose parenthood with equal respect.

This book is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental-health advice.
