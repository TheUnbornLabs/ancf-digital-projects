PROJECT 013

Time Freedom Calculator

Calculators · Difficulty: Beginner

A transparent time model. It simply does arithmetic with the hours you enter — your current day, and an optional "added daily care" amount so you can see how a bigger care commitment would change your free time. Adjust everything to your own reality; nothing is sent anywhere.

Your day

Enter values to see your estimate.

How it's calculated:

free_per_day = 24 − sleep − work − care free_per_week = free_per_day × 7 with_added_care = free_per_day − extra (per day) yearly_difference = extra × 365

How to read this

Time is not money, and this model makes no claim that one way of spending it is better. It only turns a vague feeling — "I'd have less time" or "more time" — into rough numbers you can look at. Care for a child can also be deeply meaningful time; the calculator counts hours, not value.

The "added care" figure is deliberately yours to set. Real caregiving load changes enormously with a child's age, support available, and how it's shared.

Caveats

Averages hide the texture

Care hours are rarely evenly spread; some days are far heavier than others. A daily average smooths over that reality.

Free time isn't all interchangeable

Twenty scattered minutes are not the same as a free afternoon. The total can look similar while feeling very different.

This is a thinking aid, not a verdict

Use it to make a trade-off visible, not to decide anything. People weigh time, meaning, and money very differently — all validly.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
