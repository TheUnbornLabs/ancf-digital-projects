PROJECT 006

Reproductive Autonomy Explainer

Ethics · Difficulty: Beginner · ~10 min

Reproductive autonomy is the principle that each person should be free to decide whether, when, and how to have children — including the choice to have none. It does not tell anyone what to choose; it protects the conditions under which any choice can truly be your own. It cuts both ways, equally protecting the freedom to have children and the freedom not to.

What you will learn

  • The five principles that make a reproductive choice genuinely free.
  • How to tell when autonomy is being respected versus violated.
  • Which conditions of a free choice are present in a given situation.
  • How to respond to someone's reproductive decision in an autonomy-safe way.

1. The five principles

Consent

Decisions are made with genuine agreement — never imposed or assumed.

Bodily autonomy

The final say over your own body rests with you.

Informed choice

Honest, accurate information — free of scare tactics or shaming.

Freedom from coercion

No threats, ultimatums, or guilt campaigns dressed up as concern.

Respect for disagreement

Others may disagree — and still respect that the choice is yours.

2. Scenario simulator

Pick a situation. The tool explains whether autonomy is respected or violated, and which principle is involved.

3. Rights & respect checklist

For a decision you're weighing, tick the conditions that feel true. Saved on this device — this maps where your freedom is solid and where it's under pressure.

4. Autonomy-safe response generator

5. Your reflection

Key takeaway

Reproductive autonomy is not a stance on whether to have children. It is the quiet, radical idea that the question belongs to you — and a society worth living in protects that freedom equally, in every direction.
