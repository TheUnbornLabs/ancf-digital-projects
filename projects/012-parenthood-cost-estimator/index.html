Parenthood Cost Estimator

Calculators · Difficulty: Beginner

An editable model of the cumulative direct cost of raising children. Real costs vary enormously by place, family, and choices, so treat every figure as a starting point you replace with your own. It updates live and stays entirely in your browser. This is an illustration only — and cost is just one of many things people rightly weigh.

Your numbers

Enter values to see your estimate.

What this does — and doesn't — include

It covers recurring direct costs (food, clothing, activities, day-to-day care). It does not attempt to include things that vary too much to average honestly:

  • Lost income or career impact from caregiving
  • Housing upsizing, childcare, or higher education
  • Government support, benefits, or tax credits that reduce costs
  • The many non-financial joys and challenges, which no calculator can score

So real-world figures could be meaningfully higher or lower. This is a thinking tool, not a budget.

Assumptions & caveats

Why an inflation-adjusted figure?

Costs tend to rise over the years you'd be supporting a child. The adjusted total grows each year's cost by your inflation rate, so it is usually higher than the flat figure. Set inflation to 0 to compare.

This is not a judgement on parenthood

Plenty of people consider the cost well worth it; plenty weigh it heavily. The tool takes no side — it just helps you see one part of the picture in your own numbers.

Use local figures

Average costs differ hugely between countries and households. Replacing the defaults with figures for your own situation makes the estimate far more meaningful.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
