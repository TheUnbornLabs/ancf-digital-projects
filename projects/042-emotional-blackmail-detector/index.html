Emotional Blackmail Detector

Society · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~7 min

Emotional blackmail uses fear, obligation, and guilt (FOG) as leverage. Paste a message to notice those patterns, for your own clarity. A flag does not prove the speaker meant harm — but if these are frequent and ignore your "no", that matters, and you deserve support.

An awareness aid, not a diagnosis or a verdict on anyone. If a relationship feels controlling or unsafe, please consider talking with someone you trust or a qualified professional. You deserve support.

What you will learn

  • The three levers of emotional blackmail: fear, obligation, guilt.
  • How to name a pattern without escalating.
  • When persistent pressure is worth taking seriously.

Key takeaway

Naming fear, obligation, or guilt is for your clarity — not to convict anyone. A single FOG-ish line from a loved one is human; a steady pattern that ignores your "no" is worth taking seriously, and support is a strength.
