Gaslighting Phrase Detector

Society · Difficulty: Intermediate

Paste a message to notice phrases that deny or rewrite your reality. The aim is to help you trust your own perception — it's an awareness aid, not a diagnosis of anyone, and your own sense of the situation matters most.

A flagged phrase is a prompt to reflect, not proof. Context and your own read matter.

Patterns this looks for

Minimising your feelings

"You're overreacting / too sensitive / dramatic" — treating a real reaction as excessive.

Denying events or memory

"That never happened / you're imagining it" — rewriting what you experienced.

Undermining your judgement

"You're crazy / confused / paranoid" — attacking your grip on reality.

Isolating your perspective

"Everyone agrees / no one else thinks that" — making you feel alone in your view.

Reframing harm as humour

"I was just joking / can't you take a joke?" — recasting hurt as your oversensitivity.

Trust your perception

If reading these patterns rings true for a relationship in your life, take it seriously and gently. Your memories and feelings are valid data, not flaws. Writing down what actually happened, soon after it happens, can help you hold onto your own reality. And if someone repeatedly makes you doubt your sanity, please reach out to someone you trust or a qualified professional — you don't have to sort it out alone.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
