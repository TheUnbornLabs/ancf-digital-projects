PROJECT 034

Family Function Survival Script

Writing Tools · Difficulty: Beginner

Walking into a big gathering where the questions come fast? Assemble a short, warm script you can rehearse beforehand — an opener, a deflection, a firm-but-kind line, and a graceful exit. You owe no essays.

2. Make it yours

3. Before you go

Rehearse the exit, not just the answer

Knowing your "I'm going to grab a drink" line in advance means you never feel trapped. The exit is the most useful part of the script.

Decide your topic-change in advance

Have one genuine question ready for the most persistent relative ("How's the garden / the new job / the move?"). Redirecting is easier than defending.

Pick an ally

If you can, agree a signal with a partner or cousin who'll change the subject or pull you away when you need it.
