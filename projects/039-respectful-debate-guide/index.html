PROJECT 039

Respectful Debate Guide

Community Tools · Difficulty: Beginner · ~8 min

Hard topics deserve careful conversation. This guide offers principles for disagreeing well, a quick self-audit, and phrases that help instead of harm — so you can protect both the truth and the relationship.

What you will learn

  • Principles that keep a debate honest and kind.
  • How to steelman before you rebut.
  • Phrases that lower heat instead of raising it.
  • How to audit your own debating habits.

1. The principles

Aim at ideas

Critique the argument, never the person's worth or identity.

Steelman first

State the other view at its strongest before you reply.

Stay curious

Ask questions; assume good faith until shown otherwise.

Hold your uncertainty

Be willing to say "I might be wrong" and mean it.

Know when to stop

You can exit a debate that's turning unkind. That's wisdom, not defeat.

Protect the vulnerable

Don't platform contempt for any group in the name of "debate".

2. Phrases that help vs. harm

Helps"Help me understand…" · "What would change your mind?" · "Here's where I see it differently…" Harms"You always…" · "That's stupid." · "People like you…"

Key takeaway

Good debate is a shared search, not a battle. Aim at ideas, steelman generously, stay kind, and know when to stop — and you'll change more minds, including sometimes your own.
