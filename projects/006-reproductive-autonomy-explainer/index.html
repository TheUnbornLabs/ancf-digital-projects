PROJECT 006

Reproductive Autonomy Explainer

Ethics · Difficulty: Beginner

Reproductive autonomy is the principle that each person should be free to decide whether, when, and how to have children — including the choice to have none.

The core idea

Decisions about one’s own body and family belong to the individual, made with good information and without coercion.

It cuts both ways

Autonomy protects people who want children and people who don’t. Defending one protects the other.

Coercion to notice

Pressure can be social, economic, religious, or familial. Naming it is the first step to resisting it kindly.

Reflection prompt

Where in your life do you feel most free — and least free — to make this choice?

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
