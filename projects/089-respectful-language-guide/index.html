Respectful Language Guide

Community Tools · Difficulty: Beginner

A practical guide to language that persuades without wounding. The words you choose decide whether someone listens or shuts down — and you can be completely firm while staying kind.

Principles

Be firm, not harsh

Firmness is about clarity and consistency, not volume or contempt. "This is my decision" is firm; "you're an idiot for asking" is just harsh.

Aim at the idea, not the person

"That argument has a gap" invites a reply; "you're stupid" ends the conversation. Keep criticism on claims.

Use "I" statements

"I've decided this is right for me" is hard to argue with. "You're wrong to expect that" invites a fight.

Avoid absolutes and generalisations

"Some people feel…" is accurate and fair; "everyone like you always…" is neither, and it stings.

Say this, not that

✓ Say this

"This is right for me."
"I see it differently."
"Common isn't the same as correct."
"Help me understand your worry."
"Let's leave this here for now."

✗ Not that

"Only an idiot has kids / doesn't."
"People like you always…"
"You'll obviously regret it."
"That's a stupid thing to believe."
"Whatever, you'll never get it."

Notice the pattern: the left column is about you and the idea; the right column attacks them. The left persuades; the right just vents.
