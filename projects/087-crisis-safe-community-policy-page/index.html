Crisis-Safe Community Policy Page

Community Tools · Difficulty: Advanced · ~25 min · policy builder

If you run a community that discusses heavy topics, it helps to agree — in advance — how moderators respond when someone seems to be in distress. This builds an adaptable policy template covering principles, what to do and not do, resources, and care for the mod team.

Important: This is a starting template for community conduct, not a clinical or crisis-intervention protocol, and not professional advice. Before relying on it, adapt it with input from qualified mental-health professionals and local emergency/crisis services appropriate to your members' regions. Communities support; they do not treat.

Key takeaway

You can't be a therapist for your community, and you shouldn't try to be — but you can decide, calmly and in advance, how to respond with care, dignity, and good judgement. The best crisis policy is humble: respond kindly, never promise what you can't deliver, point firmly toward qualified help, and look after the moderators too.
