PROJECT 094

Women and Reproductive Pressure

Society · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~15 min · explainer

Reproductive pressure often lands hardest on women — through an "expected path," body-clock talk, and unequal assumptions about who does the caring. This names the shapes the pressure takes and decodes the common lines, so they carry less weight. It speaks to a pattern, not a prescription for anyone.

Key takeaway

Much of the pressure on women rests on an old equation — womanhood equals motherhood — that countless women have questioned and outlived. Seeing it as a script, not a fact about you, is how it loses its grip. Your worth was never conditional on whether you become a mother.
