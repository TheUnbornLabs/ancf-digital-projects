Couple Compatibility Questions

Relationships · Difficulty: Beginner · ~9 min

Whether to have children is one of the biggest things partners can align on — and it's far better discussed early and openly than assumed. These are conversation starters, not a test. There are no right answers, only honest ones.

What you will learn

  • Questions that surface real alignment, gently.
  • Why honesty early beats assumption later.
  • How to hold the talk with care and no pressure.

1. Talk through these together

Take them slowly — one each, then compare. Mark any you've genuinely discussed. Saved on this device.

0 of 6 discussed.

2. Holding the conversation well

No pressureThe aim is understanding, not convincing. Neither answer is "wrong." Listen fullyLet each person finish before responding. Reflect back what you heard. It's ongoingOne talk rarely settles everything. Revisiting is healthy, not failure.

3. Quick check

To win the discussion

2. If partners differ on children…

Key takeaway

The healthiest couples don't assume — they ask. Talking openly about children, with no pressure and full listening, builds trust whatever you each conclude. There are no wrong answers, only honest ones held with respect.
