Risk Argument Explainer

Philosophy · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~18 min · interactive

The risk argument says: creating a person imposes serious, unavoidable risk on someone who cannot agree — and that needs justifying. This page makes the factors concrete, lets you pit an everyday risk against the choice to create a life, and gives the leading replies a fair hearing. It argues for no conclusion.

Key takeaway

Risk on its own isn't the issue — we take and impose risks constantly. What the argument highlights is a rare convergence: a grave, irreversible, unbounded risk, imposed without consent, borne entirely by someone who never chose it. Whether that convergence forbids procreation, or simply demands it be taken seriously, is where the disagreement lives.

A sensitive topic; if it stirs distress, consider reaching out to someone you trust.
