PROJECT 011

Childfree Budget Calculator

Calculators · Difficulty: Beginner

A simple, transparent estimate of what a childfree household might save and spend flexibly — and how savings could grow over time. Every number is yours to edit, it updates live, and all of it stays in your browser. This is an illustration, not financial advice.

Your numbers

Enter values to see your estimate.

How it's calculated (you can check the maths):

leftover = income − essentials annual_savings = leftover × 12 × (saverate / 100) annual_flexible = leftover × 12 − annual_savings future_value(n yrs) = annual_savings × ((1 + g)ⁿ − 1) / g · g = growth / 100

How to read this

This is a back-of-envelope illustration, not a forecast. "Flexible spending" is simply the part of your leftover you don't save — it might go to travel, hobbies, family, giving, or anything else. The projection assumes you save the same amount every year and earn a steady return, which real life never does exactly.

It is not a claim that a childfree life is "better" — only a way to see your own numbers clearly. Costs, incomes, and priorities vary enormously.

Assumptions & caveats

Why the projection is only a rough guide

It uses constant contributions and a constant growth rate, ignores inflation, tax, fees, and life's surprises. Treat the long-term figures as "ballpark", not promises.

What "growth" means here

A simplifying stand-in for investment return. Real returns vary year to year and can be negative. If you're unsure, set it to 0 to see savings with no growth at all.

Currency-neutral by design

The tool has no currency symbol — the numbers are in whatever currency you think in. Nothing is sent anywhere.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
