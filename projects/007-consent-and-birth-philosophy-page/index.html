Consent and Birth Philosophy Page

Philosophy · Difficulty: Intermediate

A recurring question in antinatalist philosophy is whether consent matters when no one exists yet to give it. This page lays out the argument and the main replies.

The argument

A person cannot consent to being born, yet birth exposes them to certain risks. Some argue this makes the act ethically weighty.

The strongest reply

Critics note that consent normally applies to existing people; you cannot wrong someone who does not yet exist. The debate turns on whether 'potential persons' have moral standing.

Why it stays unresolved

Reasonable, thoughtful people land on different sides. Holding the question openly is itself a philosophical skill.

Reflection prompt

Does consent feel relevant to you here, or does it only apply to people who already exist?
