PROJECT 057

Buddhism and Antinatalism Notes

Philosophy · Difficulty: Advanced

These notes treat Buddhism respectfully as a living tradition, exploring resonances — not equating it with antinatalism.

Dukkha

The recognition that existence involves suffering is a starting point both traditions reflect on, in different ways.

Compassion

Buddhist compassion extends to all beings; antinatalist reasoning sometimes appeals to preventing suffering. The aims can rhyme without being identical.

Important distinction

Buddhism is a vast spiritual path with its own goals (e.g., liberation), not a stance against having children. Respect that fullness.

Reflection prompt

What does compassion mean to you in this context?
