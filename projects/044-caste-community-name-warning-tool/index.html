Caste/Community Name Warning Tool

Community Tools · Difficulty: Intermediate

A safety tool for writers and moderators. Paste a draft post to check whether it names or targets a caste, religion, or ethnic group. These spaces critique pressures and ideas — never groups of people. This helps keep posts respectful and your community safe for everyone.

A flag doesn't mean your post is "bad" — naming a group can be fine in context. It's a prompt to check that you're aiming at a pressure or idea, not at people.

Target the pressure, not the people

The fix is almost always the same: replace "this group of people" with "this expectation / behaviour". Examples:

Instead of"Those people always pressure you to have kids." Try"The expectation that everyone must have kids is worth questioning." Instead ofBlaming a religion or community for pronatalism. TryNaming the specific practice or pressure, and noting many within that community disagree with it too. Instead of"All of them think…" Try"Some people argue…" — accurate, and it leaves room for the many who don't.

Why this matters here

Antinatalist and childfree spaces exist to support reproductive autonomy with dignity — including the dignity of every caste, religion, and ethnic group. Pronatalist pressure shows up across all communities, and so do people who reject it. Aiming criticism at people rather than pressure is both unfair and exactly the kind of harm this collection refuses. This tool is a small guard-rail to help keep that promise.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
