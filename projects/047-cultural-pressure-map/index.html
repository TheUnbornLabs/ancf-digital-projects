PROJECT 047

Cultural Pressure Map

Society · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~15 min · mapping tool

Reproductive pressure rarely comes from one place. This tool maps the common external sources — family, in-laws, peers, work, faith community, media, policy, society at large — so you can see where it's strongest for you. It turns a vague heaviness into a clear map, with a targeted next move for whatever weighs most.

① Rate the sources② Your pressure map③ Source guide④ Reflection

① Rate the sources

For each source, how much reproductive pressure does it put on you? (0 = none, 5 = intense.) Saved on this device.

② Your pressure map

Sorted strongest to weakest, with your overall load and a targeted move for the top sources.

③ Source guide

How each source tends to show up, and one counter-move. Tap to expand.

④ Your reflection

Saved on this device only.

Key takeaway

"Everyone wants me to have kids" is a fog; a map is a set of distinct, smaller pressures you can actually address one at a time. Some sources you can talk to, some you can limit, and some — like an advert or a stranger's opinion — you can simply let pass. Seeing them separately is what makes them manageable.
