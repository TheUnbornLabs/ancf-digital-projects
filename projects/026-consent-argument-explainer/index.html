PROJECT 026

Consent Argument Explainer

Philosophy · Difficulty: Intermediate

A focused explainer on consent in birth ethics, giving both the argument and the leading replies.

The claim

No one consents to being born, yet birth imposes lifelong stakes. Some argue this asymmetry of imposition matters morally.

The reply

Consent typically presupposes an existing subject. You cannot seek permission from someone who does not yet exist, so the framework may not apply.

A middle view

Some keep consent as a useful metaphor for taking the future person’s interests seriously, without treating it as literal permission.

Reflection prompt

Is consent the right lens here, or a different value (like care or responsibility)?
