Antinatalism FAQ

Philosophy · Difficulty: Intermediate

Antinatalism is often misunderstood. These answers aim for accuracy and respect.

Is antinatalism about hating people?

No. It is a philosophical position about the ethics of creating new life. It is compatible with deep compassion for everyone who exists.

Does it encourage harming anyone?

Absolutely not. Serious antinatalist thought concerns birth, never harm to living people. Encouraging self-harm or violence is rejected outright.

Is it the same as being childfree?

No. Childfree is a personal lifestyle choice; antinatalism is an ethical argument. They overlap but are distinct.

Do all antinatalists agree?

No — there are many internal debates. Thoughtful disagreement is part of the philosophy.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
