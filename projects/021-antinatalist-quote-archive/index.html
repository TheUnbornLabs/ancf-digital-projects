PROJECT 021

Antinatalist Quote Archive

Learning Tools · Difficulty: Beginner · ~7 min

A small archive of original study prompts that reword ideas from the antinatalist tradition for reflection. Filter by theme, draw one at random, save your favourites, and copy any to keep. These are paraphrases for learning — not verbatim quotations attributed to any author.

What you will learn

  • The recurring themes of antinatalist thought: consent, suffering, asymmetry, autonomy, ecology, compassion.
  • How a single line can compress a whole argument.
  • Which themes you return to most (shown as a chart).
  • How to keep the lines that move you.

Key takeaway

A good line is a doorway, not a proof. These prompts are meant to start your own thinking — to disagree with, refine, or carry — rather than to settle anything. Keep the ones that open something for you.
