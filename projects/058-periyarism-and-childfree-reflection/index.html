PROJECT 058

Periyarism and Childfree Reflection

Society · Difficulty: Advanced

An educational reflection on how the rationalist Self-Respect tradition associated with Periyar (E. V. Ramasamy) resonates with reproductive autonomy. It's presented respectfully and historically — and it does not claim Periyar or any community endorsed antinatalism. The shared ground is values like dignity, reason, and self-determination.

Background, briefly and accurately

Periyar (E. V. Ramasamy, 1879–1973) was a social reformer who founded the Self-Respect Movement (Suya Mariyadai Iyakkam) in early 20th-century Tamil society. It emphasised rationalism, human dignity, the questioning of inherited hierarchy and superstition, and notably women's autonomy — including support for "self-respect marriages" without traditional ritual, and for women's freedom and education.

Where the values resonate

Self-respect and choice

A tradition centred on dignity and freedom from imposed social roles resonates naturally with the idea that decisions about one's body and family belong to the individual.

Questioning custom with reason

The movement encouraged examining customs critically rather than following them by default — a useful model for thinking through reproductive expectations, while still respecting people.

Women's autonomy

Its historical emphasis on women's freedom and self-determination connects directly to reproductive autonomy in both directions: the freedom to have children and the freedom not to.

Care with representation

These notes don't claim the Self-Respect Movement, Periyar, or Tamil or Dravidian communities endorse antinatalism or being childfree — that would be putting words in others' mouths. The point is narrower and fairer: shared values like autonomy, reason, and dignity offer a respectful lens for personal reflection. For the actual history, read Periyar's own writings and reputable historians, not summaries like this one.
