3. Talking with parents

A few things that make these conversations go better. Tap to expand.

Name the love first

Starting with "I know this comes from caring about me" lowers everyone's defences. It tells them you're not rejecting them, only the topic.

Answer the fear, not the words

"Who'll care for you?" is often really "I'm scared for you." Reassuring the fear ("I'm building a secure, connected life") lands better than debating the logic.

The warm broken record

You don't need new arguments. Repeat the same kind line: "I've decided, and I'm at peace — I'd love to talk about something else." Calm repetition is the technique.

Agree to disagree

You may not change their mind, and that's okay. "We see this differently, and I still love you" can be the whole resolution.

Give it time

Acceptance often arrives slowly. A boundary held with warmth, repeated over months, usually softens things more than one perfect conversation.
