Retirement Without Children Planner

Calculators · Difficulty: Intermediate

"Who'll look after you when you're old?" deserves a real, constructive answer — and it isn't "have children." This page gives a transparent savings estimate (two ways) and, just as importantly, ideas for planning care through community and services. It's illustrative, not financial advice; consult a professional for real planning.

Savings estimate

Enter values to see your estimate.

Two ways to size the fund:

monthly_gap = desired − other A) simple drawdown: fund = monthly_gap × 12 × years B) withdrawal rule: fund = (monthly_gap × 12) / (swr / 100)

Two methods — why they differ

Method A assumes you simply spend down a pot over a set number of years, with no growth. Method B assumes your savings stay invested and you live off a sustainable withdrawal rate (the often-cited "4% rule"), so the pot can last indefinitely. Real planning sits somewhere between, adjusted for inflation, tax, and your own risk comfort.

Planning care, not children

Money is only half of later-life security. The other half is a deliberate care network.

Chosen family & friends

Invest early in deep friendships and community. Strong networks predict well-being in old age more reliably than having children does.

Professional & state care

Most elder care — even for parents — is ultimately provided by professionals and services, not adult children. Plan for and budget it directly.

Legal & medical directives

A will, a power of attorney, and an advance healthcare directive let you name who decides for you. Anyone can set these up, parent or not.

Housing & location

Choosing accessible housing near services and community, before you need it, removes a major later-life worry.

Caveats

This is a rough model, not a plan

It ignores inflation, tax, healthcare shocks, and investment ups and downs. Treat the numbers as a starting conversation with a qualified adviser.

Children are not a retirement plan anyway

Even for parents, relying on children for care is uncertain and can strain relationships. Planning your own security is wise for everyone.

This project is for education and reflection only. It is not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
