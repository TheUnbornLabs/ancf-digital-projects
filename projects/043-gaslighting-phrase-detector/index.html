Gaslighting Phrase Detector

Society · Difficulty: Intermediate · ~15 min · detector

Gaslighting is a pattern of making someone doubt their own memory or perception. This tool flags phrases associated with that pattern and offers a reality anchor for each, to help you trust your own mind. It's a reflection aid, not a diagnosis: one phrase isn't proof, context and pattern matter far more, and your read of your own experience is valid.

If you often leave conversations doubting your own memory, apologising for things you didn't do, or feeling "too sensitive," that pattern deserves real support. A trusted friend or a qualified professional can help you see clearly in a way no tool can. You are not "too much" for wanting that.

Key takeaway

Your memory and your perceptions are yours, and they’re allowed to count. Naming a technique doesn’t settle who’s right about any one event — but it can break the spell of automatic self-doubt long enough for you to check in with yourself and the people you trust. Trusting your own mind is not arrogance; it’s the ground you stand on.
