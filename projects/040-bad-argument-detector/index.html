Learn the fallacies

A reference you can read even without pasting anything. Tap each to expand.

Appeal to popularity (bandwagon)

"Everyone does it, so it's right." Popularity isn't proof. Reply: "Common isn't the same as correct."

Appeal to nature

"It's natural / unnatural, therefore good / bad." Nature isn't a moral guide. Reply: "Natural and good aren't the same thing."

Over-generalisation

"Always / never / everyone / no one." Sweeping words rarely survive a single counter-example. Reply: "Is that really true in every case?"

Slippery slope

"If we allow X, then catastrophe Y." It assumes a chain reaction without showing the links. Reply: "What actually connects X to Y?"

Ad hominem

Attacking the person instead of the argument. Reply: "That's about me, not my point — can we stick to the claim?"

Appeal to authority or tradition

"An authority / our elders said so." Worth weighing, but not automatically decisive. Reply: "What's the reasoning behind it?"
