Nietzsche Misuse Explainer

Philosophy · Difficulty: Advanced · ~20 min · explainer

Nietzsche is one of the most misquoted thinkers online — flattened into slogans to back nihilism, cruelty, or whatever the poster already believed. This explainer decodes the common misuses against closer readings, shows how the distortions happened, and tests whether famous lines are really his. The relevance here: he is sometimes conscripted to argue "life is worthless," yet he was emphatically life-affirming.

Key takeaway

A slogan is not an argument, and a quote card is not a source. Nietzsche — life-affirming, anti-antisemitic, suspicious of easy answers — is a case study in how thinkers get flattened to mean whatever a reader wants. The remedy is the same everywhere: check the context, check the source, and be wary when a complex mind is reduced to a tidy banner for your own view.
